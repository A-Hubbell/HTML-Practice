Useful Character Entities

There are a number of "reserved" characters in html such as <, >, & and non-breaking white space. There is a special way to have these characters displayed in your text as opposed to be skipped over or considered as part of tags. HTML also supports some math symbols, greek letters and other "symbols", which are not considered as character entities. Here are a few examples of character entities:

  • Ampersand (&): "&amp;"
  • Less than (<): "&lt;"
  • Greater than (>): "&gt;"
  • Copyright (©): "&copy;"
  • Registered Trademark (®): "&reg;"
